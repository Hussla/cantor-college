Learning Resources

Comprehensive library and resource centre supporting your academic journey

Library and Resource Centre

The Cantor College Library and Resource Centre is the academic heart of our institution, providing students and staff with access to extensive collections, modern technology, and quiet study spaces. Our comprehensive resources support learning, research, and innovation across all our computing and design programs.

Membership and Access

Who Can Use Our Resources

All students and staff of the College are entitled to register as members of the Library and Resource Centre and to use the facilities for the purpose of their course of study or College employment.

Membership may also be extended to other individuals and to persons nominated from other organisations: a fee is usually charged. Access to the Library and Resource Centre for study purposes may be made available to non-members by arrangement.

Member Benefits Include:

  • 24/7 access to digital resources
  • Book borrowing privileges
  • Computer and printing services
  • Study room reservations
  • Research assistance

Rules Governing the Use of the Library and Resource Centre

Introduction

Use of facilities implies acceptance of the Library and Resource Centre rules. These are designed to protect the interests of all users and to ensure that the College operates within the law and can carry out its functions efficiently and effectively.

The Library and Resource Centre rules apply in principle to all sites, including workstation rooms and site libraries. Admission is conditional upon observation of the rules and the staff are empowered to enforce them.

A. Access Rules

Educational Use

The use of Library and Resource Centre facilities is normally limited to use for the educational and other purposes of the College.

Registration

All individuals wishing to become members must comply with the registration procedures. These include an undertaking to abide by Library and Resource Centre rules.

Visitor Access

Non-members may be admitted for study purposes by arrangement. Procedures for seeking admission are outlined in the External Services leaflet. Visitors must sign the Visitors' Book on each visit.

ID Cards

ID cards issued by the College are not transferable. The person named on each card is responsible for all items borrowed and is liable for replacement costs in the event of loss or damage.

B. Use of Facilities

Personal Belongings

The Library is not responsible for loss of, or damage to, personal belongings. Users must take reasonable precautions against theft and must not leave belongings unattended.

Quiet Environment

All users are required to conduct themselves quietly throughout the Library. Conversation between users must be kept to a minimum. No conversation is permitted in designated 'Silent' areas.

Electronic Devices

Personal stereos may not be used in the Library. Mobile phones must be turned off in all study areas. Their use is permitted in the enclosed stair wells of the Main Site building.

Equipment Safety

Users should not use mains electrical supplies to power their own equipment unless it has been tested and confirmed as meeting required Health & Safety standards.

Food and Drink

Eating and drinking are not permitted in any of the public areas of the Library and Resource Centre.

Care of Facilities

Action will be taken against any person misusing furniture, facilities or materials. Any such person may be required to pay for making good damage or loss.

Our Services

Digital Resources

Access to extensive online databases, e-books, journals, and digital archives covering computing, design, and technology fields.

Research Support

Professional librarians available to assist with research projects, citation guidance, and information literacy training.

Study Spaces

Variety of study environments including silent study areas, group collaboration spaces, and private study rooms.

Technology Services

Computer workstations, printing services, scanning facilities, and access to specialized software for academic work.
